About the Author

Sado Faraji is a writer whose work explores power, belief, obedience, and resistance — the forces that shape civilizations and the quiet rebellions that reshape them.

His writing moves between epic fiction, historical reflection, and political thought. Across genres, his work asks the same question: what happens when systems demand obedience but conscience demands resistance?

Through fantasy, history, and modern political reflection, he examines authority, memory, spiritual tension, and the human cost of power.

His books are written for readers who question structures, search for meaning, and understand that history is never silent — it waits for voices willing to remember.

"I write where belief meets power, and where resistance refuses to disappear."
